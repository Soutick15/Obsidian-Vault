# Performance Review Process

**Acme Corp** | Effective January 1, 2026

## Review Cadence

Acme Corp conducts **two formal performance reviews per year**:

| Review Cycle | Period Covered | Manager Deadline | Results Communicated |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mid-Year Review | Jan 1 – Jun 30 | July 15 | By July 31 |
| Annual Review | Jul 1 – Dec 31 | January 15 | By January 31 |

New employees hired within 90 days of a review cycle are included in the next cycle.

## Rating Scale

Each employee receives a rating on a 5-point scale:

| Rating | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds all expectations; top ~10% |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Frequently surpasses goals |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Solid, reliable performance |
| 2 | Partially Meets | Inconsistent; improvement needed |
| 1 | Does Not Meet | Significant gaps; PIP required |

## The Review Process

1. **Self-Assessment**: Employees complete a self-evaluation in Workday covering achievements, growth areas, and goals for the next period. Due **1 week before manager deadline**.
2. **Manager Evaluation**: Direct managers score performance against goals and the Acme competency framework. Managers may solicit optional peer input.
3. **Calibration**: People managers meet with their department head to calibrate ratings across the team and ensure consistency.
4. **Review Conversation**: Managers deliver feedback in a 1:1 meeting. The conversation should cover what went well, development areas, and goals for the next period.
5. **Acknowledgment**: Employees acknowledge the review in Workday. Disagreements can be noted and escalated to People Operations.

## Compensation Impact

- **Annual Review ratings directly inform the annual merit increase cycle** (increases effective March 1).
- Merit increase guidelines are published by Finance and People Operations each January.
- Employees rated 1 or 2 are not eligible for merit increases during that cycle and may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).

## Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

A PIP outlines specific, measurable goals the employee must achieve within a defined timeframe (typically 30–90 days). People Operations must be involved in drafting any PIP. Successful completion of a PIP does not guarantee a merit increase.

## Goal Setting

Employees and managers align on 3–5 SMART goals at the start of each review period. Goals are documented in Workday and may be adjusted mid-cycle with mutual agreement.
